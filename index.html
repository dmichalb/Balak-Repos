About Me

I grew up in Texas but moved to Arkansas to attend the University of Central Arkansas, where I received a full-ride scholarship. I married my best friend since high school during my second year in college. He served in the Air Force and, just before my last semester of college, was moved to a base in Florida. I returned to school in Arkansas for 6 months, graduated early, and then returned to Florida where we lived for one year.

We moved back to Texas in 2018 and now own a home in Gainesville. He is currently halfway finished with his degree to become a pilot and I am in my second year of teaching middle school English.

Past Experiences

From January 2017 to December 2017, I worked at the Faulkner County Juvenile Court in Conway, Arkansas. I was a Risk Assessment Intern, meaning my job was to analyze their newly implemented risk assessment tool and determine its effectiveness in reducing juvenile recidivism rates.

During my time with FCJC, I was able to obtain research that accurately proved that the SAVRY risk assessment tool, when effectively implemented, was able to reduce the number of repeat juvenile offenders.
